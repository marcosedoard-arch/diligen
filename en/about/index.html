About Diligencir — Our Methodology and Who We Are

In the international commodity trade there is a moment when risk concentrates into a few hours: just before you wire the deposit, sign the SPA, release the shipment. That is when you are trusting documents that arrived by email, a company name you have never seen on the ground, and an intermediary who swears the product is real. Diligencir exists to light up exactly that moment.

Diligencir provides integrity and anti-fraud due diligence for the commodity trade between Brazil and the world: poultry, ICUMSA sugar, soy, corn, beef and coffee. We investigate who is on the other side of the table before the contract is yours, so that the decision to move ahead (or step back) rests on evidence, not blind trust.

Know who’s on the other side. Before the deal is yours.

What we do

Our product is the Counterparty X-Ray: a structured investigation of the company, the people behind it and the documents presented, ending in an objective verdict: GO, NO-GO or EDD.

  • GO: we found no material red flags; the counterparty and the documents held up to the verification we were able to perform.
  • NO-GO: we found indications serious enough to recommend that you not proceed as things stand.
  • EDD (Enhanced Due Diligence) — there are points that require deeper work or clarification before any decision; the signal is neither green nor red.

Every finding carries its own confidence level and its evidentiary basis. We do not hand over a loose opinion: we deliver a verdict accompanied by an evidence annex that shows where each conclusion came from. The verdict is not a guess about intentions; it is a disciplined reading of what verification revealed and of what it could not support.

The X-Ray serves both the Brazilian exporter who needs to know whether the overseas buyer is real, and the foreign importer who received an offer that looks too aggressive and wants to confirm that the meatpacker, the trading house or the intermediary truly exists and can actually deliver.

Our methodology

Diligencir’s authority does not come from a badge on the wall; it comes from the method. We work in layers, and each layer leaves an auditable trail.

1. Document analysis and light forensics

We examine the documents presented (ICPO, SCO, NCNDA, pro-forma invoices, certificates, POP, financial instruments such as SBLC) for internal inconsistencies: PDF metadata that does not match the claimed date, reused stamps and signatures, incoherent fonts and layouts, registration numbers that do not exist.

2. Multi-jurisdiction official-registry verification

We cross-check the counterparty’s identity against public and official sources in each relevant jurisdiction — among them RFB/CNPJ in Brazil, SII in Chile, Sunbiz in the US, OAB for Brazilian lawyers, CAC in Nigeria, GACC in China, and SIF/MAPA for the sanitary licensing of Brazilian meatpackers and animal-origin exporters. In a real, anonymized case we examined, the US LLC the intermediary used as a front simply could not be found in the official registry of the state where it claimed to be incorporated.

3. OSINT and asset graph

We gather open-source intelligence (OSINT) to build the relationship and asset graph: partners, addresses, phone numbers, domains, linked companies, history of presence. The goal is to see whether the counterparty has substance, or is a freshly created shell.

4. Financial and capacity analysis

We assess whether the company has the real capacity to deliver what it promises: a size consistent with the volume traded, an operating history, signs of logistical and productive structure. A seller offering tens of thousands of tonnes with no underlying capacity is, in itself, a signal.

5. Sanctions and PEP screening

We screen the counterparty and its controllers against sanctions lists and exposure as a PEP (Politically Exposed Person), within a framework of KYC, KYC-B and AML.

6. Primary-source confirmation

Whenever possible we go beyond the document and confirm at the primary source — the official registry itself, the issuing authority, the public record. A certificate is worth only what the source that issued it confirms.

7. Evidence annex with chain of custody

Each relevant element is preserved with a timestamp, source URL and SHA-256 hash. This creates a chain of custody: any reader can, in principle, retrace the path to the evidence and confirm that it has not been altered.

8. A confidence rubric per finding

Each finding receives a confidence rating that combines source reliability × information credibility. This rubric governs the language of the verdict: we write “confirmed” only when a primary source supports the finding. Otherwise we speak of an “indication,” a “sign,” or “could not be confirmed,” never more than the evidence allows.

9. LGPD compliance

We process personal data under the LGPD, with data minimization (we collect only what the purpose requires) and the lawful basis of fraud prevention. We investigate integrity. We do not dig into anyone’s life beyond what the purpose demands.

Why trust Diligencir

  • Rigor over rhetoric. No conclusion is asserted beyond what the evidence supports. The confidence rubric is what separates “confirmed” from “indication.”
  • Reproducible, evidence-backed findings. With timestamp, URL and SHA-256 hash, the evidence annex lets a third party retrace the path; the finding does not rest on our word.
  • Cautionary, indiciary language. We report indications, not moral verdicts. That discipline protects you (and us) against exposure for defamation, and keeps the report usable inside a negotiation.
  • Access to Brazilian registries that foreign buyers cannot reach. We operate in Brazil’s language, time zone and bureaucracy — RFB/CNPJ, OAB, SIF/MAPA — something an importer in Asia or the Gulf can rarely verify alone.
  • Bilingual PT/EN reports, plus an ES/ZH site. The deliverable serves both the Brazilian exporter and the international counterparty, with no translation noise in the critical conclusions.
