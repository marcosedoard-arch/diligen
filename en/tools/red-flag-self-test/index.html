Counterparty Red Flag Self-Test

Got an offer that's too good to be true, or a counterparty pushing for speed? Answer 12 questions and see, in minutes, whether the pattern matches fraud.

This self-test is educational and does not replace professional due diligence on a counterparty or transaction.

1. Does the counterparty demand buyer documents (KYC, proof of funds, LOI) before proving that the product exists?

2. Do they ask for a passport, personal data, or sensitive documents right at the start, with no clear connection to the transaction?

3. Is there deadline pressure, such as signing the SPA within 48h or losing the allocation right now?

4. Does the party making the offer claim to be the producer's mandate but offer no channel to verify this directly at the source?

5. Does any document or PDF show signs of inauthenticity (odd stamps, switched fonts, inconsistent data)?

6. Does the company or LLC involved fail to appear in an official public registry of its country of origin?

7. Do the company's economic activity or size appear incompatible with the volume offered?

8. Does the offer mix several commodities or carry certification inconsistent with the stated destination?

9. Do they ask for payment to be made to a third party's account, different from the contract counterparty?

10. Do they refuse or stall when asked to provide proof of product, such as an SGS report, dated photos, or stock evidence?

11. Is there a chain of brokers that swaps intermediaries without formalizing the change in writing?

12. Do they ask for an advance fee, commission, or SBLC before any proof of product or capacity?

Frequently asked questions

What does each result band mean?
Zero signals indicates apparent low risk; 1 to 3 signals suggests caution and Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD); 4 or more signals point to high risk, to be treated as NO-GO until independent verification. The bands are indicative and do not constitute a formal report.
Does a low result guarantee that the offer is safe?
No. The self-test covers common red flags, but no fraud is identical and signals can be hidden. Even with no Yes at all, confirm official registries, the existence of the product, and the real identity of the counterparty before closing a deal.
