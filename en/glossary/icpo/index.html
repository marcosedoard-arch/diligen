ICPO: what it is and what it is used for in trading

Updated 2026-06-19 · 2 min read
ICPO (Irrevocable Corporate Purchase Order) is the irrevocable purchase order issued by the buyer.

ICPO (Irrevocable Corporate Purchase Order) is the irrevocable corporate purchase order issued by the buyer, in which the buyer states, in a binding manner, the intention to purchase a defined quantity of a commodity under specific conditions and for a short period.

What it is

The ICPO formalizes demand on the buyer side. It usually states the product, specification, volume, target price, Incoterm, port of destination, delivery schedule, and often banking details and a validity period. The word “irrevocable” signals that the buyer commits not to withdraw the order within its validity period, which is normally quite short, a matter of a few days.

How it is used in commodities trading

In the typical sequence of a commodities negotiation, the seller issues an SCO (preliminary offer) and, where there is interest, the buyer responds with an ICPO. This document serves to demonstrate seriousness and to give the seller a basis to move toward a contract and the presentation of proof of product. In chains with intermediaries, the ICPO usually circulates together with an NCNDA and the seller-side mandate letter.

Relevance to fraud and due diligence

The key point for due diligence: despite its imposing name, the ICPO guarantees nothing on its own. It is a statement of intent to buy, not proof of funds, not a bank guarantee, and not proof that the goods exist. Fraudsters exploit precisely this appearance of formality: they use the exchange of ICPO and SCO to create a sense of progress and then demand from the buyer a guarantee, such as an SBLC, or some advance fee, before offering any verifiable proof of product. The healthy reversal of the sequence is simple: proof of product and counterparty verification come before the buyer takes on any financial commitments. An ICPO can also be used against the buyer itself, when real banking and corporate data are collected under this pretext. In an analysis, the ICPO is treated as a contextual piece, never as evidence of legitimacy.

Frequently asked questions

Does an ICPO mean the purchase is guaranteed?
No. The ICPO is a statement of intent to buy with a validity period. It does not prove funds, it is not a bank guarantee, and it does not prove the goods exist.
Why does the ICPO have such a short validity?
Because commodity prices fluctuate rapidly. A short period, a matter of a few days, keeps the order tied to a price window and pressures the parties to move forward while the conditions still hold.
Does issuing an ICPO pose a risk to the buyer?
It can. In issuing one, the buyer hands over corporate data and sometimes banking details. Without verifying the counterparty first, that data can be exploited, on top of the risk of being pressured to pay fees or open guarantees prematurely.
