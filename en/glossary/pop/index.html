POP: what Proof of Product is

Updated 2026-06-19 · 2 min read
POP (Proof of Product) is the proof that the goods exist in commodities trading.

POP (Proof of Product) is the set of evidence intended to demonstrate that the goods offered actually exist, in the declared quantity and quality, and are available for the transaction.

What it is

The purpose of POP is to answer the most basic question of any deal: does the product actually exist? In principle, this may be indicated by documents such as certificates of analysis (SGS or equivalent), independent inspection reports, warehouse certificates, proof of availability at a refinery or warehouse, or other records that can be verified independently. The value of POP lies precisely in the possibility of verification by a neutral third party, not in the mere appearance of the document.

How it is used in commodities trading

In the sequence of a commodities negotiation, after the SCO and ICPO, the buyer legitimately requests proof of product before taking on financial commitments or opening guarantees. POP serves to anchor trust in something concrete and verifiable before money or guarantees come into play. Serious operators understand the request as a normal part of the process.

Relevance to fraud and due diligence

Here is one of the most useful signals in due diligence: refusal to provide verifiable proof of product is a strong sign of fraud. When the counterparty evades, offers only documents that cannot be checked independently, demands that the buyer present an SBLC or pay fees “before POP,” or conditions the proof on signatures and confidentiality, the safe sequence has been inverted. Documents can be fabricated; that is why the POP that matters is the one confirmable at the source, with the inspector, the warehouse, or the issuing laboratory, and never through the contacts supplied by the seller itself. The absence of verifiable POP often supports, on its own, an EDD or NO-GO verdict, because without proven goods there is no real deal to protect.

Frequently asked questions

What counts as verifiable POP?
Evidence that a neutral third party can confirm at the source, such as certificates of analysis or independent inspection, reports, and warehouse receipts checkable directly with the issuer, and not just through the contacts indicated by the seller.
Why is refusing proof of product a sign of fraud?
Because the existence of the goods is the most basic fact of any deal. Evading verifiable POP, or demanding payment and guarantees before it, inverts the safe order and is one of the strongest red flags of a fraudulent scheme.
Can a POP document be forged?
Yes. That is why the value of POP lies not in the appearance of the paper, but in independent verification with the issuing laboratory, inspector, or warehouse, using official channels and not those provided by the counterparty.
